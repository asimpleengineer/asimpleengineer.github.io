II

Clear goals

"If you define the problem correctly, you almost have the solution."

Having a complete vision about what you need to do when you start a new project can be really difficult, if not impossible. Yes, you can have a vision about the end product, but the steps to get there can be similar to the moves in a chess game.

Dealing with the unknown is much like traversing a forest where you need to reach point X. Instead of trying to go directly for point X, try point A first. This "divide and conquer" approach will help both mentally and will set you one step closer to the final solution.

When planning your next step, it's okay to try or explore new things. If point A is not getting you any closer to X, don't wait to reach it; adjust your course. What's really important when doing so is to have a fast feedback loop that allows you to adapt and fallback into the right path.

Know where you want to go, plan how to get there one step at a time.
